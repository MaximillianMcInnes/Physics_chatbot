Length Contraction When objects travel close to the speed of light, to an observer moving relative to that object, it looks as if the object has become shorter This is best demonstrated using rulers Observer H, in their rocket moving close to the speed of light, measures the length of their pencil to be 14 cm Observer G, at rest on Earth, would measure (with remarkable eyesight) the length of the pencil to be shorter They will see lengths contracted in the spaceship from their reference frame, e.g. the length may appear to be 10 cm instead of 14 cm However, the same occurs the other way around For observer H on the rocket, it is observer G that is moving relative to them Therefore, observer H would measure the length of observer G's pencil as shorter i.e observer H, on the rocket, sees
