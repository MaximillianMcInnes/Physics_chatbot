This means it does not have linear or angular acceleration. For example, the torque provided by a couple on a steering wheel of radius r is Therefore, the torque of a couple is equal to double the magnitude of the torque of the individual forces The forces are equal and act in opposite directions Therefore, couples produce a resultant force of zero Due to Newton’s Second law ( F = ma ), the steering wheel does not accelerate In other words, when the force is applied, the steering wheel rotates with a constant angular speed but remains in the same location Worked Example A grinding wheel is used to sharpen the edges of pieces of wood in a school workshop. Remember, you can choose any point, not just the axis of rotation. To simplify your calculation, choose a point where the torque of (most of) the forces are unknown, or when you need to determine where the resultant torque is zero . To do this, choose a point through which the lines of action of the forces pass.
