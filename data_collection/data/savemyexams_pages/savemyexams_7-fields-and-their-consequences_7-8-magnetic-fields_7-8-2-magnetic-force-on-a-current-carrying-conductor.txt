Answer: Step 1: Write down the known quantities Magnetic flux density, B = 80 mT = 80 × 10 −3 T Current, I = 0.87 A Length of wire, L = 1.4 m Angle between the wire and the magnetic field, θ = 30° Step 2: Write down the equation for force on a current-carrying conductor Step 3: Substitute in values and calculate F = (80 × 10 -3 ) × (0.87) × (1.4) × sin(30) = 0.04872 = 0.049 N (2 s.f) Examiner Tips and Tricks Remember that the direction of current flow is the flow of positive charge (positive to negative), and this is in the opposite direction to the flow of electrons
