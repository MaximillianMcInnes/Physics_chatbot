Couples A couple is a pair of equal and opposite coplanar forces that acts to produce rotation only A couple consists of a pair of forces that are: Equal in magnitude Opposite in direction Perpendicular to the distance between them A couple must consist of two equal and opposite forces separated by a perpendicular distance Couples produce a resultant force of zero , so, due to Newton’s Second law ( F = ma ), the object does not accelerate Unlike moments of a single force, the moment of a couple doesn’t depend on a pivot The moment of a couple is equal to: Force × Perpendicular distance between the lines of action of the forces Worked Example Which pair of forces act as a couple on the circular object?
