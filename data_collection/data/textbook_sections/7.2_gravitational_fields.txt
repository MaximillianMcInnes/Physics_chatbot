Fields
Force fields
There are similarities and differences between gravitational and electric fields:
l The field strength is dependent on both the masses or charges.
l The field strength is proportional to the inverse square of their separation.
l Gravitational fields are always attractive.
l Electric fields can be attractive or repulsive.
l Electric fields are affected by the intervening medium.

Uniform and radial gravitational fields
In a uniform gravitational field the field lines are parallel and of equal separation.
In a radial gravitational field the field lines get further apart the further from the central mass (Figure 7.3).

Orbits of planets and satellites
Geostationary and geosynchronous satellites
A geosynchronous satellite has a period of exactly 1 day so that it passes over a fixed point on the Earth’s surface at the same time each day but does not remain constantly over that point.
A special case is the geostationary satellite (Figure 7.5), which gets its name from the fact that it is launched into an equatorial orbit with a period of exactly 1 day and so when viewed from any point on the Earth’s surface it remains constantly in a fixed position. This type of satellite is used for communications.

Geostationary satellites (for the Earth) have the following properties:
l	 Their orbit lies in the same plane as the equator of the Earth.
l	 The satellite period is one Earth day.
l	 They have the same angular velocity as the Earth.
l	 They move around their orbit in the same direction as the rotation of the Earth.
l	 They remain above a fixed point on the Earth’s surface.
l	 They have an orbit centred on the centre of the Earth.
